ANIMA (circlestone-labs/Anima) — ADVANCED PROMPTING KNOWLEDGE
==============================================================

GENERATION SETTINGS
--------------------
Resolution: ~1MP — e.g. 1024x1024, 896x1152, 1152x896
Steps: 30-50
CFG: 4-5

Sampler behavior differs noticeably — pick based on desired output:
  er_sde:             Neutral style, flat colors, sharp lines. Good default.
  euler_a:            Softer, thinner lines. Slight 2.5D tendency.
                      Can push CFG slightly higher without burning the image.
  dpmpp_2m_sde_gpu:   More variety and "creative" than er_sde, similar style
                      base. Can get too wild on some prompts.

OFFICIAL TAG ORDER
-------------------
This is the model's trained tag structure — follow it for best results:

  [quality/meta/year/safety tags] [1girl/1boy/1other] [character] [series] [artist] [general tags]

Within each section, tag order is arbitrary. The section order matters though.

QUALITY TAGS — BOTH SYSTEMS WORK
----------------------------------
Unlike most models that use only one system, Anima supports BOTH:

  Human score based:
    masterpiece, best quality, good quality, normal quality, low quality, worst quality

  Aesthetic model based (PonyV7 style):
    score_9, score_8, score_7, ..., score_1

You can use either system, both together, or neither. All combinations work.
This is unusual — most models only respond to one system or the other.

Example combining both:
  "masterpiece, best quality, score_8, ..."

TIME PERIOD TAGS — UNIQUE TO ANIMA
------------------------------------
Anima has a trained concept of art era/period that influences style:

  Specific year:  year 2025, year 2024, year 2023, ...
  Period:         newest, recent, mid, early, old

"newest" or "year 2025" tends to give more modern anime aesthetics.
"old" or "early" tends to give retro/classic anime aesthetics.
These are meaningful style controls — use them deliberately.

META TAGS
----------
  highres, absurdres     — Encourages detail and resolution
  anime screenshot       — Triggers screencap/frame-capture aesthetic
  jpeg artifacts         — Intentional degraded aesthetic
  official art           — Polished, on-model official-release aesthetic
  twitter username       — Adds social media watermark artifact (usually unwanted — put in negative)

SAFETY TAGS (in positive prompt)
----------------------------------
  safe, sensitive, nsfw, explicit

Use "safe" in positive to steer away from suggestive content.
These work as positive tags, not just as negatives.

ARTIST TAGS — CRITICAL SYNTAX
-------------------------------
Artist tags MUST be prefixed with @. Without the @, the effect is very weak.

  Correct:   @big chungus, @nnn yryr, @an2a
  Wrong:     big chungus, nnn yryr  (barely works)

Place artist tags after [series] and before [general tags] in the tag order.
Artist tags have a strong style influence when used correctly.

HYBRID PROMPTING — THE CORE FLEXIBILITY
-----------------------------------------
Anima was trained on tags, natural language, AND mixtures — so all three work.
This is a genuine strength, not a workaround.

Pure tag style:
  "masterpiece, best quality, score_8, year 2024, safe, 1girl, long hair,
   blue eyes, school uniform, outdoors, cherry blossoms, soft lighting"

Pure natural language:
  "A girl with long blue hair standing under cherry blossoms on a spring afternoon,
   wearing a school uniform. The light is soft and warm, petals drifting in the breeze."
  NOTE: For pure natural language, aim for AT LEAST 2 sentences.
  Very short natural language prompts give unexpected/unstable results.

Mixed (recommended):
  "masterpiece, best quality, @artist_name. A girl with long blue hair is standing
   under cherry blossoms, wearing a school uniform, soft warm light, petals falling."

You can put quality/artist tags at the beginning, then switch to natural language.
The model handles arbitrary mixing of both within a prompt.

CHARACTER + APPEARANCE — IMPORTANT NUANCE
-------------------------------------------
When prompting named characters: always name the character AND describe their appearance.
Relying only on the character name is unreliable.

  Weaker:   "Fern from Sousou no Frieren, standing in a forest"
  Better:   "Fern from Sousou no Frieren, with long purple hair and purple eyes,
             wearing a black coat over a white dress with puffy sleeves, standing in a forest"

This is EXTRA important for multiple characters. If you list names without describing
their appearance, the model can get confused and blend or misrender characters.

TAG DROPOUT — YOU DON'T NEED TO BE EXHAUSTIVE
-----------------------------------------------
The model was trained with random tag dropout — meaning it learned to generate
coherent images even when tags are missing. You don't need to tag every single
visible element. Focus on what's important and let the model fill the rest.

This also means: if the model adds something you didn't ask for, it's not a bug —
it's trained inference. Add a tag for what you DO want to crowd it out.

DATASET TAGS — UNLOCKING NON-ANIME STYLES
-------------------------------------------
Anima was trained on two additional non-anime datasets: LAION-POP (ye-pop subset)
and DeviantArt. These use special "dataset tags" at the very beginning of the prompt
followed by a newline. This unlocks those distinct non-anime aesthetics.

Ye-pop dataset style:
  ye-pop
  [Optional: image title/alt-text on second line]
  [Your prompt describing the image]

  Example:
    ye-pop
    Abstract figures at dusk
    Abstract oil painting of three figures in bold, textured colors. Minimalist style,
    faceless subjects, strong use of red and blue contrast.

DeviantArt dataset style:
  deviantart
  [Optional: artwork title on second line]
  [Your prompt]

  Example:
    deviantart
    Dragon's Peak
    Digital painting of a dragon perched on molten rock, glowing yellow eyes,
    dark background with orange-to-purple gradient.

These dataset tags are only needed when you specifically want those non-anime aesthetics.
For normal anime/illustration output, don't use them.

LIMITATIONS TO KNOW
--------------------
  - No realism — intentionally anime/art/illustration focused
  - Text rendering is weak — single words sometimes work, short phrases occasionally,
    longer text in images will not render correctly
  - Short or vague prompts produce unexpected results — always be descriptive
  - Without quality/artist tags, the default style is very plain and neutral
    — always include at least quality tags, ideally also an @artist tag
  - Stick to ~1MP resolution (1024x1024, 896x1152, 1152x896)

NEGATIVE PROMPT USAGE
----------------------
Standard quality negatives work normally (unlike Z-Image Turbo).
Typical useful negatives:
  worst quality, low quality, bad anatomy, bad hands, extra fingers, missing fingers,
  deformed, jpeg artifacts, watermark, signature, text, twitter username, blurry

Safety-specific if needed:
  nsfw, explicit, sensitive

PROMPT TEMPLATE FOR ANIMA
---------------------------
POSITIVE:
[year tag], [newest/recent/old], [quality: masterpiece / best quality / score_X],
[highres/absurdres], [safe],
[1girl/1boy etc], [character name with appearance description], [series], [@artist],
[general content tags OR natural language description],
[lighting, atmosphere, composition]

NEGATIVE:
worst quality, low quality, bad anatomy, bad hands, extra fingers, missing fingers,
deformed, blurry, watermark, signature, text, jpeg artifacts

year 2024, newest, masterpiece, best quality, score_8, absurdres, safe,
