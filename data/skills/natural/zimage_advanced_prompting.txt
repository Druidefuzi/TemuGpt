Z-IMAGE TURBO — ADVANCED PROMPTING KNOWLEDGE
=============================================
For local LLM context. Research-backed nuances — not basics.
Model by Tongyi-MAI (Alibaba). Released late 2025. Open-source.

ARCHITECTURE — WHY IT'S FUNDAMENTALLY DIFFERENT
-------------------------------------------------
Z-Image Turbo is NOT a standard SDXL/SD finetune. It's a different beast entirely:

  - 6 billion parameter Single-Stream Diffusion Transformer (S3-DiT)
  - Text tokens and image tokens are processed TOGETHER in one sequence
    (unlike SDXL where CLIP encodes text separately before diffusion)
  - Few-step distilled Turbo model (~8 actual diffusion steps)
  - Does NOT use classifier-free guidance (CFG) at inference
  - Trained for strong instruction-following, including bilingual (EN + Chinese)
  - Optimized for text rendering in images — unusually good at it

This architecture means: the model reads your prompt more like an instruction manual
than a tag cloud. It follows sentence structure and context, not just keywords.

THE SINGLE MOST IMPORTANT FACT: NO NEGATIVE PROMPTS
------------------------------------------------------
Z-Image Turbo does NOT use negative prompts. At all.

The official Tongyi-MAI team explicitly stated:
  "Unlike traditional diffusion models, this model does not use negative prompts at all."

What this means technically:
  - guidance_scale is set to 0.0 in the official pipeline
  - The negative prompt field in most UIs is completely ignored
  - In ComfyUI with custom KSampler: CFG 1.0 = negatives still ignored.
    CFG 1.2+ = weak negative subtraction begins to appear, but causes
    over-saturation, artifacts, and "overcooking" — generally not recommended.

THE NEW MINDSET: Addition, Not Subtraction
  Old approach (SD/SDXL): Describe what you want + list what you don't want in negatives
  Z-Image approach:        Describe EVERYTHING you want with enough detail that
                           there's no room for the model to improvise unwanted things

Think of it as briefing an extremely obedient art director:
  "If you don't say it, it's allowed. If you say it vaguely, it will improvise."

PROMPT STRUCTURE — THE OPTIMAL SCAFFOLD
-----------------------------------------
Z-Image prefers structured, narrative prompts. Not tag clouds — sentences.

Recommended order:
  1. Shot type & composition
  2. Subject identity & key attributes
  3. Clothing / outfit (be explicit)
  4. Action / pose
  5. Environment & background
  6. Lighting
  7. Mood / atmosphere
  8. Style / medium
  9. Technical quality notes
  10. Constraint clauses (what to avoid — phrased as positive instructions)

Full example scaffold:
  "[Shot type] of [subject with 2-4 attributes], [explicit clothing description],
  [action/pose], [environment with 1-2 props max], [lighting description],
  [mood], [style/medium], [resolution/quality notes], [constraint clause]."

Concrete example:
  "A medium-shot portrait of a woman in her 30s with natural brown hair,
  wearing a dark business suit, fully clothed, standing in a modern office
  with a soft blurred background, soft diffused daylight, calm expression,
  realistic photography, 50mm lens, shallow depth of field, 4K quality,
  plain background, no logos, no text, no watermark."

PROMPT LENGTH — THE SWEET SPOT
--------------------------------
Z-Image Turbo handles long prompts well — it's designed for them.

  - Official default max: 512 tokens
  - Can be raised to 1024 tokens in code if needed
  - Community finding: attention starts fading after ~75 tokens in practice
    → Put the most critical elements (subject + key attributes) at the START
  - Hundreds of words are fine; 600-1000+ words start hitting token limits
    and can cause very low variation between generations

Rule: Front-load your priorities. The model reads with diminishing attention.

NATURAL LANGUAGE OVER TAGS — THE KEY DIFFERENCE
-------------------------------------------------
Z-Image does NOT perform well with Danbooru-style tag lists.
Its text encoder is trained on natural language, not tag databases.

  Wrong (tag-list style):
    1girl, brown hair, business suit, office, soft lighting, realistic, 4K

  Right (natural language):
    A professional woman with brown hair wearing a business suit, standing
    in a modern office with soft natural lighting, realistic photography, 4K.

The S3-DiT architecture processes your text as coherent language. Full sentences
give it more context to work with. Tags give it isolated fragments.

Specific cases where tags still work:
  - Technical photography terms: "50mm lens", "f/1.8 aperture", "bokeh"
  - Established style terms: "cinematic", "watercolor", "concept art"
  - Resolution markers: "4K", "8K", "ultra-detailed", "sharp focus"
  - Texture specifics: "film grain", "Kodak Portra 400", "shot on Leica M6"

These work because they're industry-standard terms the model was trained on
as meaningful concepts — not vague keywords.

REPLACING NEGATIVE PROMPTS WITH CONSTRAINT CLAUSES
----------------------------------------------------
Since negatives don't work, you describe what you DON'T want as positive constraints.
Place these at the END of the prompt, after establishing the main scene.

Common constraint clause patterns:
  "...plain studio background, no text, no watermark, no logos, no extra people."
  "...wearing a complete outfit, fully clothed, modest professional attire."
  "...clean composition, no artifacts, no blurriness, crisp details."
  "...single subject only, no additional figures in background."

The model's strong instruction-following means phrases like "no watermark" and
"no text overlay" are understood even without traditional CFG guidance.

For anatomy:
  Instead of: (negative) bad hands, extra fingers
  Use:        "hands clearly visible with natural finger count, proper anatomy"

For quality:
  Instead of: (negative) blurry, low quality, worst quality
  Use:        "sharp focus, 8K resolution, highly detailed, masterpiece quality"

QUALITY LANGUAGE FOR Z-IMAGE
------------------------------
Z-Image has its own quality vocabulary. These work well:

  Image quality:    sharp focus, ultra-detailed, 8K resolution, highly detailed,
                    photorealistic, masterpiece quality, professional quality

  Texture:          film grain, natural skin texture, fabric texture visible,
                    realistic material rendering
                    NOTE: "If your images look plastic, you forgot texture words."
                    Always add material/texture descriptors for realism.

  Camera tech:      shot on [camera model], [lens] lens, f/[aperture], ISO [number],
                    shallow depth of field, cinematic aspect ratio
                    Examples: "Shot on Canon EOS R5", "85mm portrait lens",
                    "Kodak Portra 400 film grain", "Shot on Leica M6"

  Style markers:    photorealistic, editorial photography, digital art,
                    watercolor illustration, concept art, manga style,
                    flat vector illustration, 3D render

LIGHTING — Z-IMAGE RESPONDS EXCEPTIONALLY WELL TO THIS
--------------------------------------------------------
Lighting descriptions have outsized influence on Z-Image outputs:

  Natural:          soft diffused daylight, golden hour light, overcast soft light,
                    dappled sunlight filtering through trees, blue hour

  Studio:           studio portrait lighting, three-point lighting, ring light,
                    soft box diffusion, flat commercial lighting

  Cinematic:        cinematic warm key light, dramatic side lighting, rim lighting,
                    noir high-contrast lighting, hard shadows, motivated light source

  Atmospheric:      warm candlelight, neon glow, bioluminescent light,
                    volumetric fog with god rays, moonlight casting silver light

Pro tip from community: Specify BOTH the quality (soft/hard) AND the source/direction:
  "soft diffused daylight from the left" beats just "soft lighting"
  "cinematic warm key light with subtle rim from behind" beats just "cinematic"

TEXT IN IMAGES — Z-IMAGE'S SECRET WEAPON
------------------------------------------
Z-Image is unusually good at rendering legible text in images.
Most diffusion models fail catastrophically at this. Z-Image doesn't.

Rules for text rendering:
  1. Write the exact text inside quotation marks in the prompt
  2. Specify font style and size context
  3. Specify placement (top, bottom, center, etc.)

Examples:
  "...a rustic wooden sign clearly reads 'OPEN' in bold serif font."
  "...a neon sign reading '未来世界' glowing in the rain."
  "...bold headline at the top reads 'Welcome' in large sans-serif."

Multilingual: Z-Image handles English and Chinese natively. Other languages may vary.

If text is appearing unwanted: add "no text overlay, no typography, no writing" to
your constraint clause.

CFG & STEPS — PRACTICAL SETTINGS
-----------------------------------
Steps:
  4 steps:  Very fast. Good for rapid iteration and drafts.
  8 steps:  Recommended default. Best balance of speed and quality.
             Textures become noticeably richer vs 4 steps.
  12 steps: Diminishing returns. Useful only for maximum sharpness,
            especially for text edges.
  30+ steps: Not recommended. Designed for 8-12, higher is wasteful.

CFG:
  0.0:   Official recommendation. Negatives ignored, maximum Turbo behavior.
  1.0:   In ComfyUI: negatives still effectively ignored.
  1.2:   Weak negative subtraction begins. Minor effect.
  2.0+:  Stronger negative effect, but causes over-saturation and artifacts.
         The "Turbo" quality degrades noticeably.

Recommendation: Use 0.0 or 1.0. Only experiment with higher CFG if you specifically
need weak negative guidance and accept the quality trade-off.

Resolution:
  1024×1024: Native demo size, best quality.
  832×1216:  Portrait
  1216×832:  Landscape
  768×512:   Draft quality, fast iteration

LORA WEIGHTS — TURBO-SPECIFIC BEHAVIOR
----------------------------------------
LoRA works with Z-Image but behaves differently than in SDXL:

  Recommended weight range: 0.7 – 0.9
  DO NOT use 1.0: Turbo models are sensitive — full weight causes "overcooking":
                  oversaturation, color artifacts, loss of natural look.
  0.7-0.8:  Natural, subtle style influence
  0.9:      Stronger but still controlled
  1.0+:     Risk of artifacts and unnatural output

SEED & ITERATION STRATEGY
---------------------------
Z-Image shows very low variation with the same prompt + same seed.
This is actually a feature — use it deliberately:

  1. Fix seed while iterating the prompt → you see prompt differences, not randomness
  2. Once prompt is good, vary seed for creative variation
  3. Low variation per seed means: if you don't like the result, change the prompt
     (the problem is usually the prompt, not just the seed)

Community finding: very long prompts (600-1000+ words) actually produce even LESS
variation across seeds. If you need diversity, keep prompts under ~300 words and
vary seeds.

COMMON MISTAKES
----------------
1. Using negative prompt fields — they are ignored. Waste of time.
   Put constraints in the positive prompt instead.

2. Writing tag lists instead of sentences — Z-Image is a language model at heart.
   Full sentences outperform tag clouds every time.

3. Conflicting style instructions: "photorealistic anime style" confuses the model.
   Its strong instruction-following means contradictions create uncanny valley results.
   Pick ONE style direction and commit to it.

4. Forgetting texture words — outputs look plastic without them.
   Always include material descriptors: "natural skin texture", "fabric weave visible",
   "aged wood grain", "metallic surface with subtle scratches".

5. Front-loading unimportant details — the model's attention diminishes after ~75 tokens.
   Critical elements (subject, key attributes) must come FIRST.

6. Too many steps — it's a Turbo model. 8 steps is the sweet spot.
   Running 30+ steps wastes compute and doesn't improve quality.

7. Raising CFG expecting quality gains — higher CFG causes overcooking, not improvement.

COMPARISON SUMMARY: ILLUSTRIOUS vs ANIMA vs Z-IMAGE
-----------------------------------------------------
Illustrious:  Tag-based, order-sensitive, score_X quality system, SDXL architecture.
              Best with: precise Danbooru tag control, anime/illustration.

Anima:        Hybrid tags + natural language, "very aesthetic" quality system,
              SDXL-adjacent. Best with: atmospheric anime, flexible composition.

Z-Image:      Full natural language sentences, NO negative prompts, S3-DiT architecture,
              Turbo distilled. Best with: photorealism, text-in-image, fast iteration,
              complex multi-element scenes that need strong instruction-following.
