ILLUSTRIOUS XL — ADVANCED PROMPTING KNOWLEDGE
================================================
For local LLM context. No basics — only nuances and details.

ARCHITECTURE & BEHAVIOR
------------------------
Illustrious XL is an SDXL finetune with heavily expanded anime/illustration training.
It is very sensitive to tag order — what appears early in the prompt dominates the
visual composition. The CLIP encoder processes tokens in chunks, so the first "half"
of the prompt is conceptually more influential than the second.

TAG ORDER (Priority Principle)
--------------------------------
Order = weight. Rule of thumb:
  1. Quality tags (score_9, score_8_up, ...)
  2. Number of figures (1girl, 2girls, 1boy, ...)
  3. Main character attributes (hair color, eye color, body type)
  4. Clothing / outfit
  5. Pose / action
  6. Facial expression
  7. Background / setting
  8. Lighting mood
  9. Camera / composition
  10. Art style tags (if desired)

Why this matters: if you write "forest background" before "1girl", the model often
prioritizes the environment over the character. The figure then "fights" against the BG.

ATTENTION WEIGHTS — THE NUANCES
---------------------------------
Syntax: (tag:weight) — default weight is 1.0

Recommended ranges:
  - Mild emphasis:    1.1 – 1.2
  - Medium emphasis:  1.3 – 1.4
  - Strong emphasis:  1.5 (recommended maximum — above this becomes unstable)
  - De-emphasis:      0.6 – 0.9

IMPORTANT: Never use multiple strong weights simultaneously. If everything is
emphasized, nothing is emphasized. The model normalizes internally — too many
(tag:1.5) leads to artifacts and style "smearing".

Good practice:
  Wrong:   (blue eyes:1.5), (long hair:1.5), (white dress:1.5), (forest:1.5)
  Right:   (blue eyes:1.3), long hair, white dress, (forest:1.1)

Weight stacking with parentheses:
  ((tag)) ≈ 1.21  (1.1^2)
  (((tag))) ≈ 1.33
  This syntax works in Illustrious but is less precise than explicit weights.
  Always prefer explicit weights (tag:1.2) over stacked parentheses.

NEGATIVE PROMPT — NUANCES
---------------------------
Illustrious responds very well to differentiated negatives — not just quality negatives,
but also conceptual ones.

Anatomy negatives that actually help:
  bad anatomy, bad hands, extra fingers, missing fingers, fused fingers,
  extra limbs, deformed limbs, floating limbs, disconnected limbs,
  malformed face, asymmetrical eyes, crossed eyes, bad proportions

Stylistic negatives (to avoid unwanted genres):
  3d render, photorealistic   (if you want anime)
  flat color, simple background  (if you want a detailed BG)
  chibi, deformed             (if you want realistic proportions)
  sketch, lineart             (if you want a rendered output)

COMPOSITE CHARACTERS — COMMON PITFALL
---------------------------------------
With multiple figures, Illustrious tends to "blend" attributes across characters.
Countermeasures:
  - Explicitly differentiate figures: "1girl with red hair, 1boy with blue hair"
  - Use targeted attention weights: (red hair girl:1.2), (blue hair boy:1.2)
  - If needed: Regional Prompting via ControlNet or compositional aids

LIGHTING & ATMOSPHERE — UNDERESTIMATED TAGS
---------------------------------------------
These tags have disproportionate influence on overall aesthetics in Illustrious:

  Soft light:       soft lighting, diffuse lighting, ambient light
  Dramatic:         dramatic lighting, rim lighting, (spotlight:1.2)
  Moody:            golden hour, magic hour, dusk, dawn, blue hour
  Special effects:  lens flare, god rays, volumetric light, caustics
  Indoor:           candlelight, warm light, cozy atmosphere
  Cold/Mystical:    moonlight, cold light, ethereal glow, bioluminescence

Combining them makes the difference:
  Good:    soft lighting, golden hour, rim lighting
  Weak:    good lighting   (too vague for the model)

CAMERA & COMPOSITION
---------------------
Illustrious understands cinematic terminology well:

  Shot sizes:
    close-up, portrait, bust shot, upper body, full body, wide shot, cowboy shot
    (cowboy shot = from mid-thigh up, very common in anime)

  Camera angles:
    from above, from below, from behind, dutch angle, low angle, high angle,
    bird's eye view, worm's eye view

  Depth of field:
    bokeh, depth of field, blurry background, (sharp focus:1.1)
    TIP: "bokeh" alone is often enough. Adding "blurry background" reinforces it.

  Compositional rules work:
    rule of thirds, centered composition, symmetrical composition, dynamic pose

ART STYLE SPECIFICATION
------------------------
Illustrious has broad style knowledge. Precise style tags help:

  By medium:
    oil painting, watercolor, digital art, cel shading, lineart, sketch,
    comic book style, manga style, illustration

  By era/aesthetic:
    retro anime, 90s anime style, modern anime, art nouveau, ukiyo-e inspired

  Cinematic crossover:
    anime screencap, movie poster style, key visual, concept art

  WARNING: Style tags interact with quality tags.
  "score_9, realistic" leads to more realistic outputs even for anime content.
  If you want pure anime style: do not include any "realistic" tags.

CONSISTENCY TRICKS
-------------------
For consistent characters across multiple generations:

  - Always use the exact same hair color phrasing: "silver hair" — not alternating
    between "silver hair", "grey hair", "white silver hair" — this causes output drift
  - Keep outfit tags complete: not just "school uniform", but
    "school uniform, white shirt, dark pleated skirt, red ribbon"
  - Eye description: combine eye color + eye shape:
    "blue eyes, tsurime eyes" vs just "blue eyes" → measurably more consistent

CFG SCALE INTERACTION WITH PROMPTS
------------------------------------
CFG Scale (Guidance Scale) interacts with prompt complexity:

  CFG 4-5:   Very creative, hallucinating outputs. Short prompts work better.
  CFG 6-7:   Good balance. Standard for Illustrious.
  CFG 8-10:  Strict prompt adherence, but more artifacts. Long prompts OK.
  CFG 11+:   Oversteering, overcontrast — not recommended.

Rule of thumb: The more detailed the prompt, the lower CFG can go (7-8).
For creative/atmospheric prompts: CFG 6-7 allows more model interpretation.

COMMON MISTAKES
----------------
1. Quality tags placed at the end: score_9 etc. belong at the BEGINNING
2. Contradictory tags: "night scene, bright sunlight" — model compromises on both
3. Too much character detail when background is desired: BG gets drowned out
4. "beautiful" as a vague tag: better to describe concrete aesthetics
5. Duplicate concepts: "long hair, very long hair, flowing hair" — use one + weight
6. Outfit without body context: "stockings" without "legs visible" often shows no legs

ADVANCED TECHNIQUES
--------------------
AND syntax (BREAK / AND):
  Prompt1 AND Prompt2 — two separate CLIP passes, then combined.
  Useful for very different concepts that "displace" each other.
  Example: "1girl, red dress BREAK forest background, misty, atmospheric"

Prompt Scheduling (A1111/ComfyUI only):
  [tag_A:tag_B:0.5] — tag A for the first 50% of steps, then tag B
  Useful for subtle style transitions or soft attribute blending

Mind the token limit:
  CLIP has a 75-token limit per chunk (A1111 extends this automatically).
  Important tags should fall within the first 75 tokens.
  In ComfyUI: CLIPTextEncodeSDXL allows separate G/L prompts for more control.
